Case Examples

Let’s go back to the example of the e-commerce companies and analyse their dynamic solvency ratios. While Amazon has the highest proportion of its assets financed with liabilities and Zalando the lowest, the dynamic ratios show a different picture of their financial risk.

Amazon

Amazon generates high levels of operational cash flow; it is able to cover more than 80% of its financial debt with its current cash flow. If Amazon would use all its cash flow to repay its financial debt, it would need only 1.2 years to do so. This is even more remarkable, as all Amazon’s financial debt is long term. Amazon has no debt that matures within the year.

Zalando

In contrast, Zalando has a relatively lower level of operational cash flows, which makes that it can only repay 57% of its financial debt with its cash flows. More worryingly, however, Zalando can only repay 69% of its short term financial debt with its cash flows. This has led to a decrease in its cash position. In the medium term, Zalando needs to increase its operational cash flow in order to be able to repay its debt from the money it makes.

Alibaba

Alibaba’s debt repayment capacity, in contrast, is healthy: with its operational cash flow, it is able to cover its current debt obligations more than 5 times. It hence has a lot of cash left to invest – or to pay out to its shareholders – after having serviced all its contractual debt obligations.
