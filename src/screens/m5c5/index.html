PROFITABILITY: QUICK QUIZ

Firm A and B have the same level of sales and the same EBIT, but A used twice as much assets as B. How do the return on assets of A and B compare?

1

The ROA of A is half the ROA of B.

2

The ROA of A is double the ROA of B.

3

A has the same ROA as B.

Not Quite

The ROA of A is half the ROA of B, as ROA is calculated as EBIT / Assets.

While the margin of A and B will be equal, the asset turnover of A will only be half the asset turnover of B. Both together determine the ROA.

PROFITABILITY: QUICK QUIZ

Which type of company will have the highest asset turnover?

1

A retail company.

2

A steel production company.

3

A leasing company, which leases its assets on a long term basis to its customers.

Not Quite

A retail company has the highest asset turnover, as it needs less assets to operate its business compared to the production or leasing companies, which are asset heavy companies.

PROFITABILITY: QUICK QUIZ

Let's check your understanding of profitability with a quick quiz.

What actions can a firm take to increase its margin? Select all that apply.

Increase the sales price.

Negotiate longer payment terms with its suppliers.

Decrease the Bill of Materials, i.e. the cost of the materials needed to make its products.

Finance a part of its assets with debt rather than equity.

Reduce its inventory.

Not Quite

A firm’s sales price and its bill of materials directly affect the margins. The former leads to higher revenues, the latter to lower costs.

Negotiating longer payment terms and reducing inventory both improve the liquidity position, but not the margin. Finally, the financing policy does not impact a firm’s margin, which is solely driven by its operations.
