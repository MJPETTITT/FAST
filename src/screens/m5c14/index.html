RATIOS QUICK QUIZ

If AB InBev’s cost of equity equals 11.8%, did AB InBev create value?

1

Yes, because the ROE after taxes of 21.6% is higher than 11.8%.

2

Yes, because the ROE before taxes of 27.3% is higher than 11.8%.

3

No, because the ROA of 10.3% is lower than 11.8%.

Not Quite

AB InBev created value, because the benchmark is the cost of equity, which represents the after-tax opportunity cost for the shareholders. The ratio that should be compared with the cost of equity is hence the ROE after taxes, which is higher than the cost of equity.

RATIOS QUICK QUIZ

With a cost of equity of 11.8% and a high proportion of debt, the WACC of AB InBev will be...?

1

Lower than 11.8%.

2

Equal to 11.8%.

3

Higher than 11.8%.

Not Quite

The WACC is lower than the cost of equity, as debt is cheaper than equity. Debtholders bear less risk than shareholders, so they have a lower return expectation.

Liquidity Quick Quiz

Which of the following minimizes a firm’s working capital requirement? (Select all that apply)

Switching to on-line sales, rather than selling through distributors

Switching to exports to Southern European countries, where payment terms are typically longer

Increasing sales in a company with a positive WCR, while keeping DSO, DIO and DPO constant

Negotiating longer payment terms from suppliers

Not Quite

Switching to on-line sales decreases DSO and hence WCR, as customers pay immediately when buying on-line, while distributors pay after they have sold. But longer payment terms from suppliers also lead to lower WCR, thanks to higher DPO.
