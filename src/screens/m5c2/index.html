EQUITY & LIABILITIES

First, a company raises equity and debt on the right-hand side of the balance sheet to finance assets.

OPERATIONS

These assets are put to work for the company’s operations.

OPERATING PROFITS

These assets will hopefully lead to operating profits (EBIT).

DEBT HOLDERS

First, part of operating profits is allocated to debt holders as interest payments.

SHAREHOLDERS

After interests and taxes have been settled, shareholders are entitled to any remaining net profits.

RETURN ON ASSETS

The Return on Assets therefore compares a firm’s operating profit, or its EBIT, with the total amount of assets needed to generate the EBIT, as a measure of its overall operational profitability.

Note that the ROA is not impacted by how the assets are financed. It is therefore purely driven by a firm’s operations, and can be compared to that of a competitor, even if that competitor has a different financing structure.
