Three commonly used ratios to analyse a company’s WCR

From the company’s operating cycle, three commonly used liquidity ratios can be derived:

  • Days Sales Outstanding shows how many days it takes the firm to collect accounts receivable.
  • Days Inventory Outstanding reports the number of days goods spend in the company’s inventory.
  • Days Payables Outstanding shows how long the firm waits before paying for supplied goods and services.

They are closely linked to a company’s operations and business model, and together determine the working capital requirement. For example, a service company will have a very low Days Inventory Outstanding. Retailers usually have low Days Sales Outstanding and high Days Payables Outstanding. This is because their customers normally pay cash, whereas retailers receive credit from suppliers (as we saw in the Carrefour example).
