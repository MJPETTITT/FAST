EQUITY - SOURCE OF FUNDS

In return for their investment, equity investors obtain shares of ownership in the company. Ownership means that equity holders contribute to decisions, which is reflected by the voting rights they hold. Typically, the one-share-one-vote rule prevails, i.e. an investor who holds say 10% of shares will account for 10% of votes. Furthermore, shareholders are entitled to the company’s profits but also bear all losses, if any. While supplying dividends is one way to distribute profits, retaining profit within the company will increase equity value and hence the value of the shares.

DEBT - SOURCE OF FUNDS

Financial debts bear maturities, i.e., they must be repaid at due dates. Next, financial creditors receive interests payments as a compensation. They normally do not control the company.

THE CASH FLOW STATEMENT

The cash flow statement shows all cash payments to and from the company during a certain period.
