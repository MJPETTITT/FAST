YEAR 2 QUARTER 1

You now will return to the Aquasail simulation and continue to work through Year 2, Quarter 1. You will first focus on the operations: assembling and selling sailboats, and its accounting implications.

  • Collect money from customers (invoiced last quarter).

  • Pay suppliers (their invoice from last quarter).

  • Buy raw materials on credit, and receive a new invoice from suppliers.

  • Assemble 2 boats by distributing 1 unit of raw materials over each of the 2 operating Assembly Lines, and spending 500 wages per boat.

  • Pay 600 services.

You will complete all the transactions for Year 2, Quarter 1 in a single visit to the simulation, and you will be asked to develop the cash flow statement and the P&L after having completed the first quarter of the simulation. You will update the Year 2, Quarter 1 cash flow statement, and the Year 2, Quarter 1 profit-and-loss statement. Note that your year 2 cash at start figure (at the top of the Year 2 cash flow statement) is 1200. This represents the 10200 you ended Year 1 with, minus the 9000 that you just paid to the bank to pay off some of the outstanding bank debt.
