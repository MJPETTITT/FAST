WORKING CAPITAL REQUIREMENT

The formula for working capital requirement is: Accounts Receivable + Inventories - Accounts Payable

Given a company continuously conducts operations, the gap remains in the long run: there is a permanent working capital requirement. As this is economically a long term investment (although it does not appear like this on the balance sheet!), it should ideally be financed with long term funding.

Therefore, in order to reduce a company’s liquidity risk, the net working capital should be at least sufficient to cover the working capital requirement. Now we have a sound, dynamic view to assess the liquidity risk!

Optimising working capital requirement

As financing is not for free, companies try to optimise their working capital requirement in several ways, for instance:

  • Pay suppliers later
  • Optimise inventory
  • Make customers pay as soon as possible
