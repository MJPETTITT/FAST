PROFIT-AND-LOSS OR INCOME STATEMENT

A profit-and-loss statement records all revenues and expenses incurred during a period; the difference between both is the profit or loss incurred during the period.

DEPRECIATION

Depreciation represents the reduction in the value of an asset over time, due in particular to wear and tear.

EARNINGS BEFORE INTEREST AND TAXES OR EBIT

EBIT is the difference between the operating revenues and operating expenses. This is the profit generated by a company’s operations, not taking its financial revenues and costs, such as interest payments, or taxes into account.
