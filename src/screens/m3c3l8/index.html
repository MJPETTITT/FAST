COST VERSUS EBIT

Firstly, the fact the materials are not paid yet does impact the cash because nothing is paid to suppliers. It does not impact the EBIT.

Costs are recorded when resources are used. Aquasail used 4000 of materials in Quarter 3, hence they are included in Quarter 3’s production costs.

OPERATING COSTS - INVENTORY FINISHED GOODS

Secondly, Aquasail assembled 4 sailboats in Quarter 3, but sold only 2. The inventory therefore increased by 2 sailboats, the value of which is 3000. The 3000 should be subtracted from the production costs to get the cost of goods sold, which relates to the 2 sailboats that were sold.

Alternatively, note that there was an inventory of 1 sailboat at the start of Quarter 3 (left from the end of Quarter 2), which increased to 3 sailboats at the end of Quarter 3, again indicating that there was an increase in inventory equal to 2 sailboats.

OPERATING PROFIT (EBIT)

Taking everything into account, we have an EBIT of 200, which is 700 lower than that of Quarter 2.

The difference is exactly the margin that Aquasail lost by selling 1 boat less in Quarter 3 compared to Quarter 2. Indeed, the margin on 1 boat equals the sales price (2200) minus the production cost of a boat (1500) which is 700.
