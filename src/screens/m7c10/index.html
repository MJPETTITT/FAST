Danone's solvency risk is very high, as the coverage ratios are low. Its current cash flows allow to repay only 10% of its financial debt. Alternatively, it will take Danone 10 years to repay its financial debt, if the cash flows remain at the same level and all cash is used to repay debt.

Furthermore, the cash flow is just sufficient to repay the current part of the long term debt. A small drop in cash flow will jeopardise Danone’s debt repayment capacity.

ASSESSING AQUASAIL’S SOLVENCY OR LONG TERM FINANCIAL RISK

In Year 1, Aquasail’s solvency risk was very high. It’s operational cash flow was not sufficient to cover its debt obligations. Thanks to a highly improved cash flow, Aquasail’s solvency risk dropped strongly in Year 2. With the current level of cash flow, its bank debt can be repaid in approximately 3 years, and its current bank debt can be repaid from its operational cash flows. However, the current financial debt coverage is only slightly above 1. Hence, while improved, the long term financial risk remains rather high. A further improvement of profitability and cash flow generation is hence warranted for a healthy financial outlook.
