THE FULL COST METHOD

We initially used the direct cost method to value the unsold sailboat. Other cost calculation methods can be used, for example the full cost method.

In the full cost method we take into account all production costs, or 7200, and divide this cost over the number of sailboats that were assembled during Quarter 2. The full cost of a sailboat using the full cost method is therefore:

7200 / 4 = 1800
